WHAT?

To begin this project, the first circuit needed was the astable timer. In this case, astable refers to the fact that the timer circuit has no stable state, it is always fluctuating between two states in its output, on and off. The main function of this timer works by charging and depleting a capacitor. As the capacitor is charging, the output is off. Once the capacitor reaches a certain voltage, the output switches high and the capacitor depletes at about the same rate it charges with. This creates a square wave output alternating between on and off based on the relative resistance of the potentiomenter. The next clock function, the monostable clock is much more simple, essentially pulling the output high when the button closes the circuit connected to ground because of the comparator within the 555 timer. The bistable clock uses a switch to change between the astable (automatic) timer, and the monostable (manual) timer. Although a simple switch would work, another 555 timer is used to "debounce" the switch to ensure it isn't accidentally clicked multiple times at once. Finally, several logic IC's are used to choose the correct timer system to used based on the state of the switch.
