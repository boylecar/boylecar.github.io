Week 11 - Record Player

WHY?

As this week will be my last week of school this year and therefore my last project, I wanted to make it one that is meaningful and personal. My mother has an old-fashioned record player that she has never been able to get working again. For my last project, my goal was straightforward:

Fix my mom's old-fashioned record player.

HOW?

Turntables and record players that work consistently and effectively are very challenging to design and build, and would definitely be beyond the scope of a one-week project where I also need to integrate that turntable into a capable sound system. The main requirement for this project is that it's possible to put on a record and have it play through the speakers in the home audio station. The old turntable used acoustic amplification by actually capturing the sound wave directly from the needle, and sending it through a tube into the "amplifier" that worked by spinning a contraption very quickly, and forcing the sound waves to be amplified. The passive speakers were used completely independently for the AM/FM radio functions. The best solution was to replace the turntable with a new one, hook up the turntable to an audio amplifier, and put the output of the amplifier through the passive speakers.

WHAT?

Once I had decided how I was actually going to solve this problem, the solution was relatively straightforward. At the store I had to pick out a record player with a preamp to produce a PHONO output, and a compatible amplifier that will take a PHONO input and be able to drive several low-impedance passive speakers. I also needed for the output of the amplifier to have general-purpose wire outputs as opposed to any kind of specific cable, so that I could use any of the copper wire I have at home. Finally, after purchasing the turntable and amplifier, connecting them together, and wiring the left and right output from the amplfiier to the two passive speakers, I was able to create a working system for my mom's old record player. To put the finishing touches on this project, I replaced the new turntable in the same place where the old one sat and covered up the missing space.
