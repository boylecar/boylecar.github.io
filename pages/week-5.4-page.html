Week 5 - Brake Light PCB Design (Altium)

WHY?

Last year I joined Global Formula Racing (GFR), an engineering club that we have at Oregon State University. I didn't yet have the technical knowledge to really contribute as much as I would've liked, but this year I am much more confident in my knowledge and ability. This year the club is hosting a challenge for the best PCB brake light design. Because I know this would be a great way to gain more experience with circuit design and PCB design, my goal this week is to:

Design a PCB for the brake lights on the Global Formula Racing car

HOW?

The very limited amount of parameters on this design made it pretty simple, and so the design process was very straightforward. First I would begin by identifying the design requirements from the SFAE rulebook, then design the circuit in a top level diagram. After that I would need to identify the physical components I want to use out of the list of pre-approved components for GFR. Finally I would import the schematic diagram into a PCB file and lay out the PCB to meet the design specifications outlined in the rulebook.

WHAT?

The most significant design requirements for this PCB are the minimum illumination area requirement of 15cm^2, and our own requirement that the PCB take a 12V power supply from its control module. First I looked at what components were available to use, and decided that a diffused red SMD LED component was the best option. Using the datahseet I determined that it's forward voltage drop is 2V, and its suggested current is 20mA. While it might be possible then to put six of these LED's in series for a total of a 12V drop, I thought that it would be too close to the supply voltage and with a decent fluctuation in supply voltage the LED's may flicker or start to dim. For this reason I put only five LED's in series, and included a resistor that would have close to a 2V drop at 20mA (a 100 Ohm resistor). In this case, if the supply voltage did fluctuate it would maintain all four LED's and only decrease the current slightly. Next I arranged 6 series' of the five LED's and a resistor extending outward from the center of the board where 12V and GND will arrive. Finally I added a polygon ground plane to the top layer of the board.
