Week 4 - C++ Programming pt. 2

WHY?

Last week I finished briefly reviewing the course content for CS 162, the first difficult Computer Science class in the curriculum at Oregon State. I wanted to be more helpful as a peer tutor, and for my own sake I wanted to improve my C++ programming skills. This week I wanted to continue with this goal and do a few more medium-sized projects that would help me gain practical experience with C++ programming. My goal this week was to:

Apply my C++ foundation to gain practical experience with C++ programming

HOW?

Since my goal this week is to get better at C++ both for myself and to be a better tutor, I decided that the best way to accomplish both of these would be to finish the projects that students are working on for the CS 162 class right now. After looking at the document for the first two assignments, I wanted to see how I could build my own small project-- a game of blackjack that you can play through the terminal.

WHAT?

The first assignment was fairly simple and involved populating a static array representing the different locations on a basketball court where a player would shoot from for a competition. It required just 1 input from the user to know where they would like to place the "moneyball" rack, then it would fill the array with randomized results of whether the player made or missed the shots at the different locations. The second assignment involved populating multiple structs with information about teams and the players on that team that was written into a file. It took several user inputs to determine which stats to output, like the most points scored per team, or the player with the most points, etc. Finally, the Blackjack program that I created was pretty straightforward and involved handling the users input to "hit" or "stand", and then simulating the dealer's turn with randomized card selections.
